Cannabis Residual Solvent GC-FID Analyzer Application Development
Proposal for NIRA Instruments
Prepared by: Michael Lepen - Proposed Pilot Location: South Africa
Core propositionAdapt the existing fast residual GC-FID analyzer platform into a cannabis-ready residual solvent testing solution, using South Africa as a live pilot market and demonstration site. The project creates a new product application for the manufacturer and a differentiated analytical platform for the proposed cannabis testing lab.
1. Executive Summary
This proposal outlines a strategic collaboration between Africanna Analytics and NIRA Instruments to adapt, validate, and commercialize a fast GC-FID residual solvent analyzer for the cannabis and botanical testing markets.
The manufacturer already has a strong platform: a fast residual GC-FID instrument with integrated headspace capability. With a cannabis-specific column configuration, optimized headspace parameters, dedicated calibration standards, and an ISO-ready method package, the instrument can become a highly practical testing solution for cannabis extraction laboratories and independent testing facilities.
The proposed South African lab provides the ideal pilot environment: an emerging cannabis market, a real need for affordable testing capacity, and a founder with more than 13 years of GC installation, calibration, and technical field experience.
Commercial insightThe lowest-risk product opportunity is not to design a new instrument from zero. It is to repurpose and validate an existing GC-FID platform into a repeatable cannabis residual solvent analyzer package that can be sold into multiple markets.
2. Why This Opportunity Exists
Cannabis testing is becoming increasingly important as producers, extractors, and product brands move toward regulated production, quality control, and export readiness. Residual solvent testing is one of the strongest fit-for-purpose applications for GC-FID because the analytes are volatile organic compounds and FID is robust, sensitive, and cost-effective for routine hydrocarbon and solvent testing.

4. Technical Concept
4.1 Instrument Platform
The base platform is the company’s fast residual GC-FID analyzer with integrated headspace capability. The proposed adaptation focuses on method and application development rather than a completely new instrument design.
4.2 Detector
The detector remains FID. Flame ionization detection is well suited for residual solvents because it provides robust response to organic compounds, has a broad linear range, and is easier and cheaper to operate than mass spectrometry for routine solvent QC.
4.3 Column
The suggested starting column is Restek Rxi-624Sil MS or an equivalent 624-type volatile organic compound column. A practical starting dimension is 30 m x 0.25 mm x 1.40 micrometer. This phase is suitable for volatile solvent separation and residual solvent work.
4.4 Sample Introduction
The method should use the integrated headspace system. Headspace sampling reduces matrix interference, minimizes injection of heavy cannabis oils into the GC, improves system cleanliness, and makes routine workflows simpler for non-expert operators.
4.5 Cannabis Sample Preparation
A cannabis-specific sample preparation workflow must be developed for flower, extracts, oils, concentrates, distillates, resins, and potentially edibles. The SOP should define sample weight, vial size, diluent, internal standard strategy, equilibration temperature, equilibration time, agitation, and acceptance criteria.

8. Growth and Profitability Logic
The commercial appeal of this project is that residual solvent testing can be high-throughput, repeatable, and profitable when performed on a dedicated GC-FID platform. The cost per sample is relatively low compared with mass spectrometry, and the instrument can be positioned as an affordable entry point for laboratories and extraction facilities.
Profitability driver
Explanation
High instrument utilization
A dedicated residual solvent analyzer can run routine batches with minimal method switching.
Low variable cost per sample
Typical consumables are vials, septa, standards, gases, diluent, and basic prep materials.
Repeat testing demand
Extractors and producers need batch-by-batch testing, especially for concentrates and oils.
Fast turnaround premium
Customers value rapid release testing when product batches are waiting for QC clearance.
Expandable product line
The same platform concept may extend into terpenes, essential oils, botanical volatiles, and agricultural VOCs.
Sales multiplier for manufacturer
One validated application can create repeatable instrument sales across multiple customers and countries.
Investor and manufacturer insightFor the lab, the analyzer supports revenue from routine residual solvent testing. For the manufacturer, the same validated application becomes a repeatable product package that can be sold to other cannabis, botanical, and agricultural testing customers.
