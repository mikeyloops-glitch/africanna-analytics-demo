-- Sheet: README --
GC-FID Cannabis Testing Workbook – Terpenes, Residual Solvents, Optional Potency Development
Workbook purpose | FID-focused planning workbook for cannabis sample types, terpene standards, residual solvent standards, sample prep, calibration planning, and optional cannabinoid method development.
Primary FID applications | Terpene profiling and residual solvent analysis.
Optional FID development | Cannabinoid potency can be explored by GC-FID, but HPLC is normally preferred because acidic cannabinoids decarboxylate in a heated GC inlet/column unless derivatized or intentionally reported as neutral equivalents.
Legal note | Testing requirements vary by jurisdiction, license type, product category, and market. Treat the matrix as a technical planning sheet, not legal advice.
Reporting units | Terpenes and residual solvents are commonly reported as mg/g, ppm (µg/g), or % w/w depending analyte and regulation.
Two-FID setup suggestion | FID #1: headspace GC-FID for residual solvents. FID #2: liquid injection or headspace/SPME GC-FID for terpenes.
Version date | 2026-05-08
Prepared for | Internal laboratory planning / method development
CAS number note | CAS numbers were added to the Terpene Standard Components and Residual Solvent Components sheets. For stereospecific standards, confirm the CAS on the supplier certificate of analysis before purchasing/calibration.

-- Sheet: FID Sample Prep --
Workflow | Best For | Typical Prep | Vial / Injection Detail | Output / Reporting
Terpenes – solvent extraction GC-FID | Flower, pre-rolls, kief, hash, rosin, extracts, vape oils | Homogenize sample; weigh 50–200 mg flower or 20–100 mg concentrate; extract into validated solvent such as ethanol, methanol, hexane, or isooctane; vortex/sonicate briefly; dilute to calibration range; filter/centrifuge if needed. | Use standard GC autosampler vial with insert where appropriate; minimize headspace, seal rapidly, keep cool; inject liquid aliquot by split injection. | Individual terpenes in mg/g or % w/w; total terpenes = sum of quantified analytes.
Terpenes – HS/SPME GC-FID | Flower and volatile-rich concentrates where minimal solvent handling is desired | Homogenize gently; weigh validated portion, often 50–200 mg, into headspace vial; optionally add salt or internal standard depending method; equilibrate at validated temperature/time. | Use crimp/screw 10–20 mL headspace vial with PTFE/silicone septum; seal immediately to retain volatiles; sample headspace or SPME fiber. | Volatile terpene profile; good for preserving native headspace but must be validated for quantitation.
Residual solvents – HS-GC-FID | Hydrocarbon extracts, live resin, distillate, vape oil, tinctures, ethanol extracts | Weigh validated portion, often 50–500 mg depending matrix and limits, into headspace vial; add diluent/internal standard if method uses matrix dissolution; seal immediately. | Use 10–20 mL headspace vial; equilibrate under validated temperature/time/agitation; inject headspace gas to GC-FID. | Residual solvents in ppm or µg/g; compare to jurisdictional limits/specification.
Optional cannabinoids – GC-FID development | R&D only unless validated and accepted by target jurisdiction | Extract cannabinoids into solvent; consider derivatization if acidic cannabinoids must be quantified separately; otherwise GC heat converts acidic forms toward neutral cannabinoids. | Liquid injection GC-FID; higher-boiling method with inlet/column conditions suitable for cannabinoids. | Potency in mg/g or % w/w; HPLC is usually preferred for compliance potency.

-- Sheet: Calibration Plan --
Method | FID Instrument | Standard Mix | Calibration Levels | Internal Standard / QC | Primary Column/Technique | Notes
Terpenes | FID #2 | Core 18 terpene mix plus optional expanded components such as camphene and guaiol | Minimum 5 levels across expected sample range; dilute high-terpene samples to bracket calibration | Use suitable terpene or hydrocarbon internal standard not present in cannabis matrix; include continuing calibration verification | GC-FID liquid injection or HS/SPME; low-bleed nonpolar/semi-polar capillary column | Confirm resolution of pinene isomers, limonene/eucalyptol region, and late sesquiterpenes.
Residual solvents | FID #1 | Residual solvent gas/liquid mix including propane, n-butane, iso-butane, ethanol, methanol, acetone, isopropanol, pentane, hexane, heptane, toluene, xylenes as applicable | Minimum 5 levels around regulatory/specification thresholds; prepare in matrix/diluent compatible with HS | Use internal standard such as 1-propanol, t-butanol, or another validated compound absent from samples | Headspace GC-FID; gas sampling from sealed HS vial | Run blank, solvent blank, matrix spike, duplicate, and CCV. Hydrocarbon gases need appropriate certified gas/liquid standards.
Optional cannabinoid potency | Either FID after development | THC, THCA, CBD, CBDA, CBG, CBGA, CBN, CBC standards if method scope requires | Minimum 5–7 levels; high concentration range versus terp/solvent methods | Use cannabinoid surrogate/internal standard if available; derivatization QC if used | GC-FID high-boiling method; derivatization recommended for acidic cannabinoids | Not the preferred compliance approach; HPLC avoids thermal decarboxylation and better separates acidic/neutral cannabinoids.
