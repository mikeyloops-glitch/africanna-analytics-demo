# AfriCanna Analytics Website Research Notes

## Direction

The website should feel like a clean operating laboratory, not a fundraising document. The public message is:

- South African cannabis analytical testing laboratory.
- Phase 1 services: cannabinoid potency, terpene profiling, residual solvents.
- Phase 2 services: pesticides, mycotoxins, heavy metals.
- Customer outcome: sample intake, defensible analytical work, Certificate of Analysis.
- Visual style: soft white, grey, deep green, leaf green, restrained lime accents.

## Useful Market References

- Ecogreen Analytics emphasizes South African cannabis analysis, sample submission, SANAS ISO/IEC 17025 accreditation, SAHPRA licensing, HPLC, and headspace GC/MS.
- Qure shows a customer-facing service structure with CoAs, sample sizes, packaging details, and a broad menu of potency, terpenes, residual solvents, pesticides, heavy metals, mycotoxins, and physical tests.
- Vinlab Pharma positions cannabis testing as SAHPRA licensed and ISO 17025 accredited, with chemical and microbiological cannabis testing.
- PuEr Lab clearly describes instrument-method fit: UHPLC for cannabinoids, GC-MS for terpenes/residual solvents, LC-MS/MS and GC-MS/MS for pesticides, ICP-MS for heavy metals.
- Kaycha Labs and Fidelity Diagnostics provide useful examples of service-menu breadth and mass spectrometry positioning.

## Content Choices Made

- No investor language appears on the website.
- Pricing uses the internal financial model's draft launch prices:
  - Potency: R1,200
  - Terpenes: R900
  - Residual solvents: R1,600
  - Core release bundle: R3,000
  - Pesticides from R2,500
  - Heavy metals from R1,800
  - Full compliance bundle from R6,500
- Quality language is careful: the site says the lab is built around SAHPRA/SANAS pathways and ISO/IEC 17025-style controls, but does not claim licensing or accreditation has already been granted.
- Generic icon sets were avoided. Instrument visuals are custom CSS line diagrams and logos are custom SVG assets.
